## Before

Recent advances in transformer-based architectures have significantly improved the performance of natural language processing systems across a wide range of tasks. These models are capable of learning contextual representations from large-scale datasets and can be fine-tuned for downstream applications.

## After

Transformer architectures have reshaped much of modern NLP because they learn context-sensitive representations at scale. After pretraining, the same model family can often be adapted to narrower tasks through fine-tuning, although performance still depends heavily on data quality, task design, and evaluation setup.
